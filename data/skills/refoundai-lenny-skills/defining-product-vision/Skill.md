# Product Vision Creation

Design an aspirational future state that inspires teams and guides strategic decision-making.

Help the user with product vision creation using insights from 22 guests and posts across Lenny's Podcast and Newsletter.

## How to Help

1. **Define the Destination** - Guide the user in articulating a lofty yet realistic future state grounded in a potent user problem.
2. **Craft the Narrative** - Help translate technical goals into an emotional metaphor or a strategic narrative that resonates with human psychology.
3. **Visualize the Outcome** - Advise on creating visual prototypes or storyboards that make the abstract vision concrete for stakeholders.
4. **Connect to Execution** - Facilitate the process of working backward from the ideal future to create a tactical multi-year roadmap.

## Core Principles

### Use Emotional Metaphors
Ami Vora: "If we all agree that the feeling of something should be, I'm sitting in Dolores Park with my friends on a sunny Saturday, then people will just naturally build something that feels more consistent."

Establish a shared emotional metaphor to guide decentralized teams toward consistent decision making without constant oversight.

### Ground Vision in Potent Problems
Ebi Atawodi: "So four things. So it has to be lofty, it has to be realistic, it has to be devoid of any tech or limitations of today, and it has to be grounded in a very clear and potent problem. User problem."

Ensure the vision is rooted in a clear and significant user problem that the team feels personally motivated to solve.

### Synthesize Across Functions
Mihika Kapoor: "You want to always ensure that there are research insights that help you feel what a user is feeling. You want to ensure that there are beautiful designs and prototypes that help communicate what this future world looks like, and you also want to root it in engineering and feasibility. And you want to be constantly, even in the vision phase, assuring that what you're marching after is something that is achievable and something that you can work towards."

A compelling vision must be a cross-functional blend of deep user empathy, technical feasibility, and visual storytelling.

### Balance Art with Science
Sanchan Saxena: "If you become too scientific, you miss out on opportunities because you can't see those opportunities using the scientific method of discovery, because they're too tiny or they're so farfetched that people cannot even understand them."

Avoid over-reliance on scientific methods and A/B testing to prevent missing out on far-fetched or nascent product opportunities.

### Unify with a Strategic Narrative
Andy Raskin: "The one thing I say is it's this one story that the CEO uses to drive success in marketing, sales, but also product. That it becomes like a north star, strategic north star for product roadmap, for fundraising, for recruiting, really everything."

Develop a single high-level story that acts as a North Star to align every department from sales to engineering.

### Default to Large-Scale Thinking
Shreyas Doshi: "Google, I think the main thing I learned was the power of thinking really big. And I know it sounds like a platitude, but really big. And I only actually realized that when I left Google and I started working with the other teams and these were all capable teams and I was struck by how many teams just limited the potential of what they could achieve."

High-performing cultures prevent teams from setting artificial limits by making large-scale thinking a default operating habit.

## Questions to Help Users

- "What specific feeling do you want users to experience when they interact with your product?"
- "If all technical and operational constraints were removed, what would a 15-out-of-10 experience look like?"
- "What is the potent user problem that makes this vision necessary right now?"
- "What does the world look like after your mission is successful in ten years?"
- "Can you describe your product's destination using a non-technical analogy?"
- "What are the non-negotiable strategic shifts that must happen to achieve this vision?"

## Common Mistakes to Flag

- **Incremental Strategic Drift** - Teams often focus on short-term resource availability rather than defining an ideal future state and working backward.
- **Over-Reliance on Data for Vision** - Strict adherence to scientific methods can cause leaders to miss nascent opportunities that require artistic intuition.
- **Text-Heavy Vision Documents** - Lengthy text-based descriptions are harder to consume and align on compared to visual prototypes and vivid storytelling.
- **Disconnecting Vision from Daily Tasks** - Sustained motivation is lost when employees cannot see a clear link between their immediate work and the company mission.
